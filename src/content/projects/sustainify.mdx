Sustainify was a project where I focused on building software around a real-world problem rather than just technical novelty. The goal was to make sustainability more actionable for users by turning abstract concerns into something interactive, understandable, and useful inside a web app.

From a portfolio perspective, I would position Sustainify as a product-thinking project: one where I had to balance usability, scope, and implementation speed. I worked through how to present information clearly, shape the user flow, and craft an experience that felt practical instead of purely conceptual. That made it a good exercise in translating an idea into something people could actually navigate and evaluate.

What I learned from Sustainify was how important framing is in product development. Even when the underlying idea is strong, the project only lands if the interface makes the value obvious quickly. I also learned how to make tradeoffs under time pressure and how to ship a coherent MVP instead of overbuilding.
